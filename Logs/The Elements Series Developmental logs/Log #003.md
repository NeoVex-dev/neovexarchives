###### **#The story across the whole series**

The main story I developed looked something like this:



&#x09;In Elements: Discovery, all the ball has ever seen is this house it stayed in and its world outside of that was completely black. It 	feels lonely and doesn't have much to do, and starts exploring. The ball eventually discovers the world out there and progresses through 	layers and levels, eventually reaching the final level. At the end of the game, the ball has to interact with an object (like a button) 	and suddenly this really cinematic transition occurs and the 2d world becomes 3d. 



&#x09;In the next game, Elements: Layers, it starts following the events of elements: discovery showing the 2d-3d transition, and then hands 	the controls onto the player. As the player continues to explore the world the player begins to notice glitches and weird things 	happening in the environment. In the events of Elements: Layers, however the player never finds out why, it just notices something is 	wrong as the player progresses it catches a few leads and in the end of the game it finds a final lead. 



&#x09;The next game, Elements: Motion, starts with the player following the lead but in the end reaches another dead end early in the game. 	The rest of the entirety of the game the ball gives up, begins to think its paranoid and takes a step back to enjoy its environment. 	Throughout the game, the ball doesn't notice anything unusual until the end. Towards the end of the game the sky begins to glitch as it 	the ball was in an artificial biome and ends with terrain collapsing around it



&#x09;Elements: Preservation begins with the same cutscene that ended Elements: Motion and then the player is put in the events of 	preservation. As the player progresses through levels they gather more information. The sole motivation to continue is the urge of 	survival and the need to know what's going on. Towards the end of the game, the player understands it is currently in a containment 	unit. The game ends once the player finishes the levels and reaches the end where the artificial environment gets wrecked and 	destruction is all around.



&#x09;The final game, The Elements revolves around the need to escape containment, enduring the elements.



Unfortunately, an ending for the entire series has not been established yet, but once it has been pondered and decided I will discuss it in a future log.



I've carefully chosen every detail for the structure of the series. I was hoping for all the games to seamlessly transition into each other story and experience wise. I'm currently working on trying to discover and understand what i really want for my first game (Elements: Discovery) before i continue writing the overall cross-game stories and conclusions, so i don't rush anything. In fact this entire story was made as a began to understand what i wanted for my first game, and if i don't think of a conclusion by the time i finish this game, I'll have to wait, It should come naturally. I hope this gave you an insight of my main plan for the structure of this game series
