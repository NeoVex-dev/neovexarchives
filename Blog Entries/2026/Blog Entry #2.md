**The GDD felt too rigid for me — here's what I came up with instead**



Published Date:  07/06/2026



Hey, I recently decided to document my game. I started with some research and learned about the main documents like the GDD and TDD, and decided to jump right in. After thinking about what I should write for my GDD and digging deeper into how they're supposed to be written, I felt not only lost, but also like the structure was too rigid. 



Eventually, I decided to create my own document structure rather than follow the traditional GDD structure. While I was conceptualising what my document would look like, I decided I needed a few principles to be prioritized when writing the document. This included atmosphere, cinematics, progression, and most of all, the player experience. After a little research and brainstorming, I decided to name the document the GECPD, or the Game Experience Cinematics and Progression Document.



While it doesn't have the best name, this document structure helps the developer maintain a clean and intuitive structure while understanding the game through the player's journey. It serves as a comprehensive master document that combines multiple documents rather than keeping separate documents for GDD, atmosphere, player experience, lore bible, and cinematics, and mixes them into one master document. 



Of course, this doesn't eliminate the need for essential dedicated documents, like in my case, separate from the GECPD, I keep a style guide, features list, and high-concept brief, along with level-design notes, rather than every level having a separate explanation in level-design docs.



Let's go a little deeper into the differences between a GDD and a GECPD.



| Aspect | GDD | GECPD |

|----------|----------|----------|

| Structure | UI, Art Style, Levels, Audio, and Basic Story | Combines these parts of the game and displays them as the player experiences them |

| Organization | Organized based on systems and categories | Organized based on how the player experiences it |

| Main Question | How are the systems in the game designed? | How does the player experience the game? |

| Atmosphere | Often described in dedicated sections or supporting documents | Described based on how the player views it and how the game progresses |

| Cinematics | Described in a dedicated section of the GDD | Integrated throughout the document |

| Lore | Often outlined in the GDD and expanded through supporting documents | Written as the player encounters and understands the lore |

| Progression | A single section of the GDD | The core of the document structure |

| Purpose | Dedicated to planning and designing systems that are part of the game | Designing and understanding the overall game flow and progression based on how the player experiences it |

| Note | — | The GECPD is not intended to replace all traditional game development documents. Instead, it acts as an experience-focused master document that works alongside documents such as style guides, feature lists, technical documents, and production plans. |

0.3 Title Screen
